Accountants and bookkeepers do very different things for the companies they work for. Bookkeepers are responsible for the ongoing maintenance of their clients' general ledgers, such as compiling data on a daily basis, categorizing expenses in the general ledger, reviewing the general ledger for accuracy, reconciling bank statements, and generating financial statements, but accountants use the bookkeeper's accurate and up-to-date general ledger to provide advisory services such as analyzing the company's financial data and preparing income tax returns as well as tax planning advice.

Small businesses don't require the services of a local bookkeeper. The internet and cloud technology enable bookkeepers to securely access their clients' books at any time and from any location. Instead of wanting to hire the local, community bookkeeper by default, business owners can now choose a bookkeeper who actually understands their industry and operations. Our certified bookkeepers serve all time zones in the United States and are readily available.
